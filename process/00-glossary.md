# 00 GLOSSARY — Binding Terminology

## Rule
- **Terms from established dramaturgy** (Hutzler, McKee, Truby, etc.) → **English, as-is**
- **Terms from our process** (original concepts) → **English, defined below**
- All agents use exclusively these terms. No synonyms, no translations, no alternatives.

---

## Dramaturgical Terms (from Hutzler / established dramaturgy)

| Term | Source | Definition | DO NOT use |
|------|--------|------------|------------|
| **Ghost** | Hutzler | The formative experience that made the character who they are today. Source of the Lie. | ~~Wound~~, ~~Trauma~~, ~~Backstory~~ |
| **Lie / World View** | Hutzler | The character's false belief about how the world works. A coping strategy against the Core Fear. In Hutzler's eBooks, found under "World View". | ~~False belief~~, ~~Misbelief~~ |
| **Want** | Established | The character's conscious, active, external goal. Drives the A-Story. | ~~Outer goal~~, ~~Desire~~ |
| **Need** | Established | The character's unconscious inner deficit. Drives the B-Story. | ~~Inner need~~, ~~Flaw~~ |
| **Fears** | Hutzler | The character's deepest fear — the fear beneath the fear. The secret conviction about themselves that must be hidden at all costs. | ~~Deepest fears~~, ~~Anxieties~~ |
| **Dark Side** | Hutzler | The character's behavior under maximum pressure — their darkest version. Concrete actions when all strategies fail. | ~~Shadow~~, ~~Dark version~~ |
| **Life Lessons** | Hutzler | What the character would need to learn to fulfill their Need and overcome the Lie. | ~~Lessons~~, ~~Growth target~~ |
| **Arc** | Established | The character's transformation over the narrative. Positive arc = Lie recognized. Negative arc = Lie deepened. | ~~Journey~~, ~~Development~~ |
| **Paradox** | Hutzler | The ONE trait that makes the character simultaneously admirable AND destructive. Strength = Weakness. | ~~Contradiction~~, ~~Duality~~ |
| **Story Questions** | Hutzler | The questions that drive the character through the plot. | ~~Dramatic questions~~ |
| **Character in an Instant** | Hutzler | Who the character is when you first see them. Essence in a moment. | — |
| **Turning Points** | Established | The 2-3 key moments of inner transformation, each triggered by another ensemble character's action. | ~~Plot twists~~ |

## Process Terms (our concepts)

| Term | Definition | Origin |
|------|------------|--------|
| **Value Conflict** | Two genuine human values in tension. The core of the entire narrative. | Theme phase |
| **Facet** | A position / perspective within the Value Conflict. Each facet becomes a character. | Theme phase |
| **Idea Battle** | The argumentative conflict that runs through the entire narrative via the Value Conflict. Characters EMBODY positions in this battle but don't DESCRIBE it. The Idea Battle happens in ACTIONS, not DIALOGUE. | Theme phase |
| **Message** | The author's position in the Idea Battle. Embodied through the outcome of the action. | Theme phase |
| **Crucible** | The mechanism by which pursuing the Want actively prevents fulfilling the Need. Want ⇄ Need opposition. | Character phase |
| **Here and Now** | Who the character is when we experience them in the narrative. Appearance, habits, quirks, contradictions. | Character phase |
| **Anti-Value** | Destructive principle (hatred, greed, etc.) that is the Dark Side of a real value. Forbidden in the Value Conflict, welcome in characters. | Theme phase |
| **Value Conflict Tension** | Every character carries BOTH sides of the Value Conflict — even if they primarily embody one facet. The opposing side lives in them as inner tension, fear, or suppressed need. NOT identical to Paradox. | Character phase |
| **Collision Pair** | Character pair where A's Want sabotages B's Need. Creates recurring, unsolvable friction. Marker: ⚡ | Ensemble phase |
| **Core Find** | Research material with maximum dramatic potential for a character's Crucible. Marker: ⚡ | Research phase |
| **Recombination** | Creative transfer of a structural principle from a reference work to our material. Operations: Cross, Invert, Radicalize, Transplant. | Synthesis Engine |
| **Facet Compass** | The facet gives the character a DIRECTION in the Idea Battle, not a TRAIT. The character stands for a position — but LIVES it through concrete existence, never EXPLAINS it. | Character phase |
| **Invention** | The creative act of creating concrete humans, scenes, details that GO BEYOND the derivation chain. The chain is the FOUNDATION — it gives direction and psychological depth. The output must be MORE than the sum of inputs. | Character phase |
| **Concreteness Mandate** | From the character phase onward: No abstract output. Every phase has its PERSPECTIVE. **CHARACTERS:** From the character's POV — what do they do, how do they experience the world? **WORLD:** Concrete places with boundaries and power dynamics. **STRUCTURE:** Beats — moments of change, not summaries. Test: Could this sentence appear in an essay? If yes → rewrite. | Process-wide |

## Medium-Specific Terms

### TV Series
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Season Arc** | A character's transformation across one season |
| **Episode** | Single narrative unit within a season |
| **A/B/C Story** | Storyline hierarchy within an episode (~50-60% / ~30-40% / ~10-20%) |
| **Serialization Degree** | How much carries over between episodes (horizontal vs. vertical) |
| **Cliffhanger** | End-of-episode decision point (crossroads, not action cliff) |

### Film
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Three-Act Structure** | Setup / Confrontation / Resolution |
| **Sequence** | Mini-film within the larger structure (Gulino: 8 sequences) |
| **Midpoint** | Greatest external success AND greatest internal loss |
| **Climactic Choice** | The protagonist's final, defining decision |

### Game
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Quest** | A narrative unit with goal, obstacle, and resolution |
| **Branching Point** | Where player choice creates divergent narrative paths |
| **Hub World** | Central location connecting multiple narrative threads |
| **Lore** | World-building information discovered through exploration |
| **Narrative State** | The accumulated consequences of player choices |

## Application Rule for All Agents

Use EXACTLY the terms in the left column — not alternatives. These terms remain in English even in non-English prose:

- "The character's **Fears** drive every decision."
- "The **Arc** runs negative."
- "The **Dark Side** manifests in concrete actions."
