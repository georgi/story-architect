# 08 PITCH — Pitch Distillation

## Purpose

Distill the entire project into a compelling pitch document, logline, and tagline. Characters and world in foreground. Plot only teased. Theme shines through, never named.

---

## Prerequisites

- All prior wiki entities should exist (theme, characters, world, tone, format)
- `wiki/author-voice.md` — Author's voice must be present in the pitch

---

## Step 1: Pitch Document

Five parts, in this order. Each part has a specific function.

### Part 1: META
Title, genre, format, tonality. One block, no prose. Pure information.

```
Title: [TITLE]
Genre: [GENRE]
Format: [MEDIUM — episode count, runtime, serialization]
Tone: [Pitch Tone from tone.md]
```

### Part 2: OPENING
Emotional entry. The reader must FEEL something before they understand anything.

- Start with an image, a moment, a sensation — not with exposition
- Establish the emotional register of the entire narrative in 3-5 sentences
- Make the reader curious, uncomfortable, or moved — before they know a single character name
- This is the author's voice at its purest

### Part 3: WORLD
Make the Conflict World visible. Hint at sub-worlds.

- Describe the world as a character would experience it — sensory, not encyclopedic
- Establish the rules: what is possible, what is forbidden, what is at stake
- Show the power dynamics without naming them
- Sub-worlds as glimpses — enough to suggest scope, not enough to exhaust it
- 4-6 sentences maximum

### Part 4: CHARACTERS
Protagonist + 2-3 key ensemble characters.

For each character:
- Who they are when we meet them (Here and Now) — 1-2 sentences
- What they want (Want) — stated or implied
- Why we can't look away (Paradox or Crucible) — the hook
- NO backstory, NO psychology, NO Enneagram types

Characters in the pitch are ALIVE — people you want to watch, not case studies.

The protagonist gets the most space. Supporting characters get 1-2 sentences each.

### Part 5: ACTION TEASER
Tease the narrative movement. Do NOT tell the story.

- What is the initial situation? (Setup)
- What disrupts it? (Inciting incident)
- What is at stake? (Stakes)
- Where could this go? (Promise — but don't deliver)
- End with a question, an image, or a tension — NOT with a resolution

**Critical rule:** The pitch sells the EXPERIENCE, not the plot. The reader should feel "I need to see this" — not "I know what happens."

---

## Step 2: Logline

**Maximum 50 words.** Contains:
- Protagonist (who — by trait, not name)
- Want (what they pursue)
- Theme (implicit — the Value Conflict as felt tension, never named)

**Format:** When [situation], [protagonist] must [action] — but [obstacle/tension].

**Test:** Does the logline create a question the reader wants answered? Does it suggest the Value Conflict without stating it?

---

## Step 3: Tagline

**Maximum 10 words.** The core tension of the Value Conflict, compressed into a phrase.

- Not a summary — a provocation
- Not a question — a statement or fragment
- Must create tension in the reader

**Test:** Would this work on a poster? Does it make someone stop scrolling?

---

## Principle

| Foreground | Background | Absent |
|-----------|------------|--------|
| Characters + World | Plot (teased) | Theme (felt, never named) |
| How it FEELS | What HAPPENS | Why it MATTERS (implicit) |
| Author's voice | Structure | Process terminology |

---

## Quality Gate

Before completing pitch, verify:

- [ ] Logline ≤50 words, contains protagonist + want + crucible tension
- [ ] Tagline ≤10 words, captures Value Conflict as felt tension
- [ ] Pitch structure complete (all 5 parts present)
- [ ] Characters and world in foreground — not plot
- [ ] Plot only teased — no resolution revealed, no full arc described
- [ ] Theme FELT, not STATED — no mention of Value Conflict, facets, or thematic vocabulary
- [ ] Author's voice recognizable — consistent with `wiki/author-voice.md`
- [ ] Opening creates emotion before information
- [ ] No process terminology in the pitch (no "Ghost," "Lie," "Facet," etc.)
- [ ] Would a producer/publisher/player want to see more after reading this?
