# Tone — The Iron Pass

## Narrative Tone
The tone of a chronicle written by someone who was there and chose not to intervene. Cold observation that makes warmth unbearable when it arrives. Conversations where the silence between words carries more weight than the words themselves.

## Dialogue Style
- **Default Mode:** Spare, transactional. People trade information the way they trade iron — by weight, not by sentiment.
- **Pressure Mode:** Fewer words. Then one too many.
- **Intimacy Mode:** Doesn't exist. Closest thing: two people sitting by a fire without speaking.
- **Forbidden Register:** Earnest declarations of feeling. Nobody says "I trust you" in the Iron Pass — they show up when the tunnel collapses.

## Pacing
- **Scene Tempo:** Long establishing shots of the mountain, the fortress, the trade road — then hard cuts to faces in firelight
- **Escalation Pattern:** Slow compression. The pass narrows. The snow thickens. Then a gate opens — and what's outside is worse than the cold.
- **Breathing Room:** The mountain at night. Wind. Distant wolves. Nobody speaking.
- **Time Signature:** 5/4 — always slightly off-balance, like walking on frozen scree

## Visual/Sensory Language
Desaturated palette. Northern winter light — iron-gray sky, black stone, firelight orange in the dark. Camera that watches from a distance, then suddenly too close. Deep focus on the fortress and the pass, shallow focus on faces. Sound design: wind and stone underneath everything, the mountain breathing.

## Reference Matrix
**Core Formula:** "Game of Thrones meets Name of the Rose, in a fortress where the stone remembers everything"

### Touchpoints
1. Game of Thrones — specifically: power as a corrosive force shown through character erosion, not spectacle
2. Name of the Rose — specifically: institutional investigation in a closed world where the institution is the disease
3. Macbeth — specifically: the fortress as psychological prison, the throne that devours whoever sits in it

### The Original Move
The mountain itself is a character with moods — snowfall, rockslides, the mines groaning at night. The pass dictates when trade moves, when armies march, when people starve. Time is measured in snowdrifts.

## Pitch Tone
A northern epic where the fortress that protects the kingdom is run by the person most likely to destroy it — and the law sent to stop her is the thing that will make her worse.
