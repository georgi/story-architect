# Serana Blackthorn

## Ghost
Serana was twelve when her father knelt in the courtyard of Ironhold. Lord Harren had him executed for organizing the miners — not for rebellion, for asking that the tunnel supports be replaced before the spring collapse killed another dozen men. They made the families watch. Her mother stood behind her and covered Serana's eyes, but not her ears. The sound an axe makes on a block is not the sound you expect. Afterward, Lord Harren sent the family a purse of silver — compensation. Her mother threw it down the mine shaft. They ate roots that winter.

## Lie
If you hold the power, you choose who suffers. Serana runs the fortress the way the mountain runs the weather — impersonally, totally, without appeal. She calls it protection. She protects people the way a cage protects a bird: by making the sky irrelevant.

## Want
Absolute control over the Iron Pass — every toll, every mine shaft, every grain wagon, every sword that crosses the mountain answers to her.

## Need
To recognize that she has become Lord Harren with a different justification. That her father wasn't killed because nobody held power — he was killed because the person who held power didn't see miners as people.

## Crucible
Serana's Want (control) prevents her Need (recognition). Every time she tightens her grip — taxing merchants, silencing dissent, punishing disloyalty with exile into the winter — she proves to herself that power only flows downhill. The control that avenges her father IS the thing her father died fighting.

## Fears
**Core Fear:** Being powerless when it matters. Not weakness — weakness at the moment someone she loves needs her to be strong.
**Secret Shame:** She let the old steward Maren freeze in the lower cells for three days before she gave the order to release him. He'd questioned her toll increase publicly. He lived. He doesn't speak in council anymore. She tells herself it was necessary. She checks on him through the window of his quarters more often than she admits.

## Dark Side

### Stage 1: Vigilance
Serana walks the walls at night. Checks the gate locks herself. Reads the miners' shift reports for patterns. Knows which families are buying more food than usual. This looks like diligence.

### Stage 2: Testing
She gives her captains conflicting orders and watches who questions, who obeys, who tells the others. Small fractures she engineers to map the fault lines. "I told you the grain store was sealed. Why does Aldric know it's open?"

### Stage 3: Leverage
Serana begins collecting debts — not coin, but secrets. Who owes whom. Who drinks too much. Whose children are hostage to their parents' obedience. The ledger she keeps in her chamber has nothing to do with trade.

### Stage 4: Isolation
She dismisses her inner council one by one. Eats alone in the upper hall. The fortress still functions — it functions because everyone is afraid to let it stop. She mistakes their fear for her competence.

### Stage 5: The Siege
Serana closes the pass. Not because of the enemy — because of her own people. She would rather starve the fortress than let anyone inside it act without her permission. The mountain becomes a prison, and she is both warden and inmate.

## Life Lessons
That the miners who come to her with complaints about tunnel supports are not threatening her authority — they are doing exactly what her father did, and she is answering the way Lord Harren answered. That when she exiles a family for speaking in council, their children watch from the courtyard the same way she watched at twelve. That protecting people means letting them refuse her protection — letting Maren disagree in public without three days in the cold, letting the grain merchants set their own prices even when she knows better. That the people inside the fortress are not ore to be extracted and refined into obedience. That releasing control of the pass does not mean her father's death was meaningless — it means his death meant exactly what he intended it to mean.

## Paradox
Serana seized power to prevent another Lord Harren. She has become Lord Harren — but she executes with exile instead of axes, and she tells herself that makes her different. It doesn't.

## Here and Now
38, lean and angular, dark hair pulled back tight enough to show the scar above her left ear where a rockfall caught her in the mines at sixteen. Wears boiled leather and iron rings — no silk, no color. Hands calloused from years underground before she climbed above. Lives in the upper hall of Ironhold, the lord's chambers she stripped bare — stone walls, a single fire, a campaign table covered in maps and ledgers. Drinks hot water with nothing in it. Speaks as if every word costs her something. Has a habit of touching the courtyard stones where her father knelt, early mornings, when she thinks the watch has changed and nobody is looking.
