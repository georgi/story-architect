# Aldric Vane

## Ghost
Aldric's mentor at the High Court — Magistrate Oren Fell — sold pardons for six years. Aldric didn't see it. They shared chambers. They argued law by candlelight. When the King's Inquisitors came, Aldric was cleared but reassigned to the Northern Circuit — the Crown's way of saying "you're clean but you're marked." Oren was hanged. Aldric was given a mountain posting nobody wanted.

## Lie
If you follow the law faithfully, justice follows. Aldric clings to statutes, precedent, proper procedure — because the alternative is admitting that his mentor fooled him for six years and his judgment about people is worthless.

## Want
To build an airtight case against the Iron Pass's corruption through proper legal channels, proving that the King's law reaches even the highest fortress.

## Need
To accept that his devotion to procedure is terror of trusting his own instincts again — and that sometimes justice requires acting before the law catches up.

## Crucible
Aldric's Want (prove the system works) prevents his Need (trust himself). Every time he discovers evidence that the legal channels are compromised — that Serana has bought the magistrates below, that the Crown's own tax collectors take their cut — he doubles down on procedure instead of adapting. The more corrupt the system reveals itself to be, the more rigidly he follows its rules.

## Fears
**Core Fear:** Being deceived by someone he respects. Not being wrong — being wrong about a PERSON he chose to trust.
**Secret Shame:** He had suspected Oren. One evening, a merchant's wife came to their chambers weeping, and Oren sent her away with a sealed letter. Aldric saw the letter. Aldric chose not to ask. That evening is the day Aldric actually failed — not the day the Inquisitors knocked.

## Dark Side

### Stage 1: Over-documentation
Aldric documents everything. Audiences get transcribed. Messages get copied before delivery. He sketches the layout of rooms he's visited — in case something changes. This looks like thoroughness.

### Stage 2: Verification loops
He checks testimony twice. Then three times. Then rides to confirm what he already confirmed. Cases slow to a crawl. The Crown calls it "Northern caution." It's paralysis wearing diligence as a mask.

### Stage 3: Accusatory pattern recognition
Aldric starts seeing betrayal in ambiguity. A guard's shifted schedule is suspicious. A missing ledger page is sabotage. He doesn't accuse — he WATCHES. And the watching makes him see things that aren't there.

### Stage 4: Isolation through distrust
He stops sharing case progress with his clerk. Works alone by candlelight. His wife's letters go unanswered for weeks. He tells himself he's protecting her from the case. He's protecting himself from needing anyone.

### Stage 5: The Violation
Aldric fabricates a witness statement. One document, one time, to move a case that procedure has strangled. He tells himself it's justice. It's the exact thing Oren did — choosing the outcome over the process. Aldric has become the corruption he was sent to root out.

## Life Lessons
The High Court taught Aldric that proximity blinds you. The Northern Circuit is teaching him that distance doesn't sharpen your vision — it just makes you cold. He hasn't learned this yet.

## Paradox
Aldric's obsession with legal procedure — his armor against being fooled — makes him unable to see the truth when it arrives outside the law. The thing protecting his judgment has replaced his judgment entirely.

## Here and Now
34, tall, gaunt from the mountain cold he hasn't adjusted to. Still wears Southern court dress in a fortress town — the only person in the Iron Pass who owns a fur-lined cloak that's never been near an animal he killed himself. Wedding ring he turns when calculating. Rents a room above the chandler's shop he hasn't furnished — his law books are still in their travel chest. Cooks elaborate meals from Southern recipes with ingredients that don't exist this far north, substitutes badly, eats standing up. Rides south every month to see his wife, arrives exhausted, leaves before dawn. Has started walking the fortress walls at first light — tells himself he's learning the terrain.
