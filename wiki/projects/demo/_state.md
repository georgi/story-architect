# Project State — The Iron Pass

**Last reviewed:** 2026-04-09
**Reviewed by:** Critic Agent

## Strongest Element

Serana Blackthorn's character file is the best work here. The Ghost-to-Paradox chain is airtight -- every link earns its place, and the Dark Side ladder delivers concrete, escalating actions (walking walls at night, engineering conflicting orders, collecting secrets in a ledger, dismissing council, closing the pass) that a writer could shoot tomorrow.

## Biggest Problem

The project has only two characters and no supporting cast. Two people cannot sustain a TV series. The Collision Pair between Serana and Aldric is described as a "methodological war," but without secondary characters who embody Facets 3 (Corruption) and 4 (Loyalty), the Value Conflict has nowhere to go except back and forth between two positions. The theme defines four facets; the cast covers two. Half the thematic argument is uncast.

## Generic Alarm

**1.** Tone file: "The tone of a chronicle written by someone who was there and chose not to intervene. Cold observation that makes warmth unbearable when it arrives." -- This is literary-catalogue prose. It sounds like a book jacket written by an MFA program. The author's actual voice would say something like: "Protokollton. Der Erzahler kommentiert nicht. Wenn Warme kommt, tut sie weh."

**2.** Tone file: "Time Signature: 5/4 -- always slightly off-balance, like walking on frozen scree" -- Clever-sounding but meaningless. What does 5/4 time signature mean for a TV series structurally? This is the kind of metaphor that impresses in a pitch meeting and delivers nothing in a writers room.

**3.** Theme Message: "Power seized to protect becomes indistinguishable from the tyranny it replaced. Law followed without judgment becomes the instrument of injustice. The Iron Pass destroys anyone who tries to hold it with one hand." -- The third sentence is a tagline masquerading as thematic argument. "Destroys anyone who tries to hold it with one hand" is vague. What does holding something with one hand mean? This is the kind of sentence that sounds profound without committing to a position.

**4.** Ensemble file: "Both want the Pass to serve its people -- Serana through control, Aldric through law. Their methods are incompatible but their goals overlap dangerously." -- "Goals overlap dangerously" is generic conflict-description language. HOW do they overlap? What specific situation makes the overlap dangerous? The sentence avoids the concrete.

**5.** Serana's Life Lessons: "She decided to become the mountain -- and the cost was becoming something that can't bend, can't grieve, can't be moved by anything softer than iron." -- The tricolon ("can't bend, can't grieve, can't be moved") is AI-pattern writing. The author's voice uses lists of CONCRETE THINGS, not abstract qualities. Should be a specific moment or behavior, not a poetic summary.

## Prioritized Recommendations

1. **[URGENT]** Create 2-3 supporting characters to carry Facets 3 (Corruption) and 4 (Loyalty). The project cannot advance to structure without them. Start with Brynn (already named in world.md) and at minimum one figure inside Serana's power structure whose loyalty is the thing that breaks. Agents: `philosopher` (facet assignment), then `ghost-lie` + `want-need` + `manifestation` (character creation).

2. **[URGENT]** Create `format.md`. A TV series with no episode count, no runtime, no serialization model is a concept without a container. The format determines everything downstream -- how many arcs, how many collision pairs per episode, what the A/B/C story split looks like. Agent: `format`.

3. **[HIGH]** Rewrite both characters' Life Lessons sections. Currently both restate the Lie rather than naming the unlearned lesson. Per glossary: Life Lessons = "What the character would need to learn to fulfill their Need and overcome the Lie." Serana's should name what she needs to learn (that protection requires releasing control). Aldric's should name his (that judgment requires trusting himself, not the statute). Agent: `ghost-lie`.

4. **[HIGH]** Reconcile tone.md with author-voice.md. The current tone file is written in a literary-epic register that contradicts the author's established voice (laconic, concrete, milieu-grounded, no atmospheric indulgence). The Iron Pass needs a tone document that sounds like it was written by the same person who wrote "In einer Kleinstadt halt man zusammen" -- not by someone pitching a prestige fantasy show. Agent: `tone`.

5. **[MEDIUM]** Rewrite Aldric's Here and Now to lead with behavior, not physical description. The author's voice explicitly forbids appearance-first character introductions. "34, tall, gaunt from the mountain cold" should become what Aldric DOES -- the cooking, the unfurnished room, the law books still packed. The behavioral details are already there; they just need to come first. Agent: `manifestation`.

6. **[MEDIUM]** Expand the Theme Message to address BOTH poles of the Value Conflict. Currently it diagnoses Serana's trajectory (power becomes tyranny) and gestures at Aldric's (law becomes injustice) without equal weight. The Message should be the author's position on the RELATIONSHIP between Control and Trust, not a warning about one side with a footnote about the other. Agent: `philosopher`.

7. **[LOW]** Add an exterior sub-world to world.md -- the trade road, the caravan camp, the Southern Kingdom's presence. The pressure cooker needs an outside force. Without it, the pass is a closed system with no external stakes. Who starves if the pass closes? We need to see them, or at least see their emissaries. Agent: `world-builder`.
